Abdul Sattar Edhi's

Abdul Sattar Edhi was a renowned Pakistani philanthropist, social worker, and humanitarian, widely respected for his tireless efforts to help the poor and needy. Born in 1928, he dedicated his entire life to serving humanity without any discrimination of race, religion, or background. He founded the Edhi Foundation, which became Pakistan's largest and most trusted welfare organization, offering services like free hospitals, orphanages, homeless shelters, and ambulances. Despite his massive impact, Edhi lived a simple and humble life, wearing plain clothes and never seeking fame. His compassion and dedication earned him national and international recognition, and he remains a symbol of hope and humanity for millions.

Biography

  • Full Name: Abdul Sattar Edhi
  • Date of Birth: January 1, 1928
  • Place of Birth: Bantva, Gujarat, British India
  • Migration: Moved to Karachi, Pakistan after the partition in 1947
  • Early Inspiration: Witnessed poverty and suffering, which inspired him to help others
  • Founded: Edhi Foundation in 1951
